HydroBattery Atlas - Help

What is Pumped Hydro Energy Storage?

Pumped hydro stores energy by moving water between two reservoirs at different elevations. When electricity is abundant, water is pumped uphill. When electricity is needed, water flows downhill through turbines to generate power.

Configuration Types

  • Lake Pair: Two natural or artificial lakes at different elevations connected by a tunnel/penstock.
  • Lake-Ocean: An upper reservoir on elevated terrain near the coast, using the ocean as the lower reservoir.
  • Underground: A surface reservoir paired with an underground cavern or aquifer.
  • Mine Void: Repurposed open-pit or underground mines (like Kidston in Queensland, Australia).

How to Use

  1. Search: Navigate to an area of interest, set your energy target and parameters, and click "Find Sites".
  2. Browse: Explore the database of known pumped hydro sites worldwide.
  3. Manual: Place upper and lower reservoir markers on the map for custom evaluation.

Cost Estimates

Cost estimates are preliminary (+/- 50%) and based on published engineering cost models from NREL, ORNL, and industry data. Actual costs depend on geology, environmental factors, labor markets, and regulatory requirements.

Data Sources

  • Elevation: Open-Meteo API (Copernicus GLO-90, 90m resolution)
  • Geocoding: OpenStreetMap Nominatim
  • Map tiles: OpenStreetMap / OpenTopoMap
  • Cost models: NREL ATB 2024, ORNL Hydropower Baseline Cost
