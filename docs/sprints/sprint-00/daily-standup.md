# Daily Stand-up — Sprint 0

**Time:** 09:30, 15 minutes, time-boxed by the Scrum Master.
**Format:** stand up (literally), no laptops open except the sprint board on screen.
**Facilitator:** will-scrum-master-alpha (Mon/Wed/Fri); will-scrum-master-bravo (Tue/Thu, with a Charlie-lens).
**Audience:** Squad Alpha members + Frontend Tenant-UX (for S0-07). Observers welcome (PO, Tech Lead, Programme Manager) but must not interrupt.

---

## Agenda (rotate speakers clockwise)

For each engineer, **three sentences**:

1. What I finished yesterday that moves a sprint board column.
2. What I will finish today that moves a sprint board column.
3. What is in my way.

Anything beyond three sentences goes to a **parking lot** the Scrum Master captures and addresses after the stand-up.

---

## Scrum Master closing (90 seconds)

- Read out impediments captured today.
- Name the two PBIs nearest the right edge of the board (closest to Done).
- Name the one PBI not yet started but on the critical path (S0-01 → S0-04 → S0-05 → S0-06).
- Confirm or clarify the sprint goal.

---

## After-stand-up huddles (optional, time-boxed)

The Scrum Master can call up to **two** five-minute huddles immediately after stand-up for blockers that involve only a subset of the team. Anyone not in the huddle disperses.

---

## Anti-patterns the Scrum Master watches for

| Anti-pattern | Intervention |
|---|---|
| Status report to the manager | Redirect: "Tell the team, not me." |
| Solving the problem in the room | Park it. Schedule a huddle. |
| Silence from one engineer three days running | One-on-one same day, before lunch. |
| "I am blocked but it is fine" | Treat as red. Pull on the thread. |
| Stand-up running over 15 min | Hard stop at 15. Anything outstanding goes to the huddle. |
| Engineers talking past each other on terminology | Tech Lead writes a one-line definition in the ADR repo same day. |

---

## Tuesday and Thursday Charlie-lens (will-scrum-master-bravo)

On Tue and Thu, the Bravo SM facilitates with an explicit Charlie focus:

- Frontend Tenant-UX gives an additional sentence on i18n coverage and a11y score.
- Cross-squad dependency check: anything Charlie needs from Alpha that is not yet visible on the Alpha board.
- Procurement-cycle reminder when relevant: "Reminder — DIANA cohort call submission window opens in N weeks. Sprint 0 outputs we want in the demo reel: …"
