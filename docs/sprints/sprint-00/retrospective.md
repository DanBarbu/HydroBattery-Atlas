# Sprint 0 — Retrospective (Day 14)

**Duration:** 90 minutes total.
**Facilitator:** will-scrum-master-alpha (lead) + will-scrum-master-bravo (Charlie perspective).
**Audience:** delivery team only — no PO, no PM, no observers. Psychological safety is the headline.
**Format chosen for Sprint 0:** **Sailboat retrospective** (good for a forming team — visual, low-pressure, surfaces both energy and drag).

---

## Pre-retrospective check (5 min)

The Scrum Master:
- Reviews working agreements (be hard on ideas, soft on people; one mic; no laptops; phones face down).
- Confirms anonymity option for written cards.
- Posts the sprint goal and the acceptance state from yesterday's review at the top of the board.

---

## 1. Set the stage (10 min)

**Prompt:** "In one word, how does Sprint 0 feel as it ends?"

Round-robin. Scrum Master writes the words on the board without comment. This is a temperature read, not a discussion.

---

## 2. Gather data (25 min)

**Sailboat board:**
- 🌬️ **Wind in our sails** — what propelled us this sprint?
- ⚓ **Anchors** — what slowed us down?
- 🪨 **Rocks** — what risks lie ahead, visible but unaddressed?
- ☀️ **The destination** — what does success look like for Sprint 1?

Silent writing for 10 minutes (sticky notes or digital cards). Then dot-vote (3 dots per person across all four columns).

---

## 3. Generate insights (20 min)

Take the top 5 voted items. For each, ask:

1. What happened? (just the facts)
2. Why did it happen? (use 5 Whys, lightly)
3. What would we like to be different next sprint?

Scrum Master keeps this on the rails — no scapegoating, no design debates. Surface the systemic cause, not the individual fault.

---

## 4. Decide what to do (20 min)

From the insights, pick **at most three** improvement actions. Each must have:

- A one-sentence description.
- An owner (a person, not a role).
- A success measure (what changes by Sprint 1 retro).
- A start-now or schedule-now flag.

Three is the limit. More than three improvement actions in a sprint is theatre.

---

## 5. Procurement / interoperability look-ahead (5 min)

will-scrum-master-bravo, briefly:
- Next funding-instrument deadline relevant to the team (DIANA / NIF / EDF / EDIRPA / MApN Innovation Cell).
- Any conformance / interop deadline (FMN Spiral, STANAG, AQAP).
- One ask: is anyone on the team interested in attending a related event in the next two months?

This is a cultural drumbeat. The team is building defence software; staying current is part of the job.

---

## 6. Close (5 min)

**Prompt:** "In one sentence, what is the one thing you will personally do differently in Sprint 1?"

Round-robin. Scrum Master notes any commitments that touch shared work.

---

## Output artefact

The Scrum Master commits to the repo (under `docs/sprints/sprint-00/retro-output.md`):

- The sailboat board (photo or markdown).
- The three improvement actions with owners and success measures.
- A note on the temperature read (the round-robin words from §1).

The retrospective output is **team-internal**. The Scrum Master extracts only what the Programme Manager needs (impediments to escalate, risks to log) and shares that separately.

---

## Anti-patterns the Scrum Masters watch for in this retro

| Anti-pattern | Intervention |
|---|---|
| One person dominating | Switch to silent-writing format mid-retro |
| "Everything was fine" — anchor column empty | Probe with: "Anything that you wanted to fix mid-sprint but didn't?" |
| Improvement actions are vague aspirations | Reject. Owner + measure + deadline, or it does not go on the list |
| The same anchor recurs from a hypothetical earlier sprint | Surface the systemic pattern; consider escalating |
| Silence from a junior engineer | Ask them directly the next morning, one-on-one |
| Leaking out of the room | Reaffirm psychological safety in writing in the channel |
